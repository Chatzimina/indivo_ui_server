Data Protection and Cookies Notices

 

  1. General Information

The iPHR platform is an online system that offers users the opportunity to record, gather, edit, add to, store and share their personal health information. With the iPHR tools, users can control their health record, including how to share their health information with third persons they choose such as their health care provider, family members or friends. They could also permit their data to be used for research. The service provided by iPHR aims to provide novel mobile and web-based tools for patients with cancer to empower and assist them in managing their disease. More information on all the features of the iPHR and the service it renders can be found in the user’s manual[1]. This data protection notice will inform you about the processing of your data within the iPHR system and the security measures that have been taken to protect your privacy. Please note that health data is sensitive data, consider carefully before sharing it.

The Foundation for Research and Technology - Hellas (FORTH) is the controller of the personal data processed within the iPHR system, unless otherwise stated. You can contact us through the following address:

  1. How your data will be collected

Most of the personal information processed within the iPHR system is collected through the information the user provides directly for the following purposes:

  1. Creating a user iPHR account;
  2. Recording or keeping user information in the application as the user provides or wishes;
  3. Pairing a sensor.

The legal basis for processing this data is Art. 6 (1) (a) of the GDPR—consent of the user. This consent is obtained before concluding the registration as a first-time user.

  1. Other data not directly provided by the user

Other information that will be collected which is not directly provided by the user is information needed to operate and improve the service, particularly, how the user interacts with our services. This includes: 

  1. Your IP address,
  2. Date and time of access,
  3. Name and URL of the retrieved file,
  4. Website from which access is made (referrer URL),
  5. The browser used and, if applicable, the operating system of your computer as well as
  6. The name of your access provider.

The data mentioned from No.2 – No. 6 above are only collected when the user access the iPHR platform through the website. All data not directly provided by the user will be processed for the following purposes:

  • Ensuring a smooth connection of the website,
  • Ensuring a comfortable use of our website,
  • Checking and guaranteeing system security and stability, as well as
  • For administrative purposes.

The legal basis for processing this data is Art. 6 (1) (f) of the GDPR—our legitimate interest. Our legitimate interest follows from the purposes listed above for data collection. In no case shall we use the collected data for drawing conclusions about your person.

All the above-mentioned data will be processed only to the extent that they are necessary to provide the services we offer, and as described in this privacy notice. The following elaborates further on the information that the user provides directly.

  1. Data collected while creating an iPHR account

Creating an iPHR account is required before using the application. To create an account, a first-time user will be required, after downloding the app or assessing the website, to provide personal information such as

  • name and surname*,
  • place of residence,
  • role,
  • cancer type,
  • sharing preferences,
  • pilot, and
  • e-mail address*.

The reason for requesting your name and surname is to uniquely identify you, while your place of residence is relevant for psycho analysis. The role is to categorize users to match what they could or could not do in the platform, while the cancer type is relevant for grouping of users so as to provide them with recommendations matching their cancer type. The sharing preferences is to know whether the user wants to share data for research or not or to do so on a case-by-case basis, while the e-mail address will only be used for the verification of your account, notification in case of a change to this privacy notice and for generating a new password in case you forget the one you currently have. If you lose your account credentials, you can follow the lost password procedure, and a new password will be sent to your email. Note that this password is temporary and should be updated at your next login to the system.

Only the information about name and email is mandatory, others are optional. You can review and update your account information at any time you choose by updating your profile or others apps.

  1. Data collected during the use of the iPHR application

You will choose what kind of health information to put in your record. Examples of the type of information you can enter, view subsequently and update include information on:

  • eDiary - Calendar: A timeline view of all available information showing medications, problems, appointments and procedures.
  • Demographics: Patient information such as gender, date of birth, contact information, name and surname.
  • Labs: Laboratory result values.
  • Problems: Diseases, illnesses, injuries, physiologic, mental or psychological condition or disorders.
  • Procedures: Medical treatments or operations of the patient.
  • Allergies: Abnormal reactions to encountered allergens.
  • Medications: Drugs or other substances received.
  • Measurements: Vital signs that indicate the status of the body’s vital functions.
  • Contact: A form to communicate with other users of the system.
  • Appointments: Appointments between the patient and doctors.
  • Upload Documents: Health data documents.
  • Clinical Assessment App: A questionnaire for identification of a more effectively care for patients.
  • Psycho-Emotional Status Monitoring: Questionnaires will be filled by patients to monitor their psych-behavioral status.
  • Family Resilience Evaluation Tool: Questionnaires will be filled by patients/family members to monitor their family resilience status.
  • Personal Health Information Recommender: Allow patients to search in a high-quality document repository for useful information

The user manual referred to above also contains information on all the apps provided by the iPHR and the kind of data you can put in your record, and could be consulted by the user as well.

  1. Pairing a sensor

Life style and vital sign measurement data can be collected and monitored with the help of (off-the-shelve) sensors and medical devices. It comprises the measurement of the body weight and patient’s vital signs (blood pressure, pulse, body temperature) as well as the tracking of patient’s activity. Results can then be used in dependent scenarios such as patient self-management scenarios. The portal provides an API for storing and collecting vital signs such as blood pressure (diastole and systole), body temperature, weight and pulse. In order to receive or send data through the API, the application should use OAUTH 2.0 provider in order to authenticate and be authorized through tokens and SSL protocol.

  1. Sharing your health information

The user can share health information with other users or groups called “carenets”. There are three default groups (carenets): Work/School, Physicians and Family. In each of these groups, the user can add as many users as he/she desires. The user will be able to specifically pick those apps and documents stored in those apps that he/she wants to share. When authorized by you, those persons mentioned in your carenets will only be able to view your data. They cannot change or delete information in your record. It is only a carenet assigned the role of a “Guardian” (eg, parents) that could modify or download data.

Because of the risk to your privacy when inappropriate access is grated to your information, please consider all the consequences carefully before you grant access to any third parties. Particularly sensitive data can be kept under special restrictions for access; the user can block his account simultaneously blocking all data access to all his/her information.

 

          VIII.    Data Processor

We use Amazon AWS private virtual machine as hardware and network provider and therefore engages Amazon as a data processor. Our processor relationship is governed by a contract that mandates the processor to process data only as authorized by FORTH.

                    IX.      Data transfer to third parties

The Provider does not transfer user data to third parties. Only to obey a legally binding court order or for law enforcement purposes shall we share relevant information with these authorities.

                    X.       Data retention

As long as the user is using the services, his or her data will continue to be processed. Once the account is deleted, we do not retain any data about the individual. Data is also deleted from the back up, except previously anonymized data. Archieved and log data are automatically deleted after six months.

                    XI.      Deleting your account

You can close your iPHR account at any time by signing in to the service, go to settings and choose the option of delete account. When you close your account, all your data will be deleted. Other users that you have given any level of viewing access to your health information will not be able to see your record any longer.

 

                    XII.     Deleting health information

When the user moves a piece of health information to the trash, the user can either restore it or delete it permanently from there at any time. Other users with whom the deleted health information has been shared will not be able to see or restore items in the recycle bin, nor can they permanently delete your health information. Permanently deleting health information removes it from the recycle bin. Once an item has been permanently deleted, it cannot be restored. The service adds an entry in your record indicating the date and time that the deletion occurred.

 

                    XIII.    Rights of the data subjects

Under EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), data subjects within our platform have the following:

 

  1. Right of information

You have the right to know the information concerning the data processing that involves your personal data in a transparent way. See Articles 13 and 14 of the GDPR for more about this right.

  1. Right of access

You have the right to ask us for copies of your personal information. Although this right always applies, there are some exemptions, which means you may not always receive all the information we process. See Article 15 of the GDPR for more about this right.

  1. Right to rectification

You have the right to always ask us to rectify information you think is inaccurate. You also have the right to ask us to complete information you think is incomplete. See Article 16 of the GDPR for more about this right.

  1. Right to erasure

You have the right to ask us to erase your personal information in certain circumstances. See Article 17 of the GDPR for more about this right.

  1. Right to restriction of processing

You have the right to ask us to restrict the processing of your information in certain circumstances. See Article 18 of the GDPR for more about this right.

  1. Right to object to processing

You have the right to object to processing if we are to process your information because the process forms part of our legitimate interests or public interest. See Article 21 of the GDPR for more about this right.

  1. Right to data portability

This only applies to information you have given us. You have the right to ask that we transfer the information you gave us from one organisation to another, or give it to you. The right only applies if we are processing information based on your consent or under, or in talks about entering into a contract and the processing is automated. See Article 20 of the GDPR for more about this right.

  1. Right not to be subject to a decision based soley on automated processing

You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects concerning you or similarly significantly affects you. See Article 22 of the GDPR for more about this right.

 

According to Article 77 of the GDPR, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in particular in the Member State of your habitual residence. For instance, the Hellenic Data Protection Office, Kifissias 1-3, 115 23 Athens, Greece, E-mail: contact@dpa.gr, Website: http://www.dpa.gr/.

  1. Freedom to withdraw consent

Apart from the rights mentioned above, you are also free to withdraw your consent to the processing of your data at any time without providing a reason for your decision. Your decision to withdraw your consent will not have any negative impact on you. In the case of withdrawal, personal data will be immediately deleted and no longer be processed by the iPHR system. Any data that could be reconnected to you will be deleted. Please note that personal information that was anonymised in the course of the operations cannot identify you and therefore will not be deleted.

For questions concerning the collection, processing or use of your data, as well as to claim your rights, please contact us through the address at the beginning of this notice.

You are not required to pay any charge to the iHPR platform for exercising your rights, and we may have up to two weeks to respond to you.

                    XIV.   Security of your personal information

  • encrypting (ssl) the communication between the browser and the server;

Furthermore, data will be stored in one virtual machine hosted on a public cloud (Amazon Web Services) with the following characteristics:

  • encrypted storage, the uploaded documents from the patients will be stored encrypted on the server

The virtual machine will use the latest version of Operating System with auto updates for security reasons.

We also use appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect your data against accidental or intentional manipulation, partial or complete loss, destruction or unauthorised access by third parties. We continually adapt our security measures in line with technological developments.

                    XV.     Automated Decision-Making

The Profiler tool, analyses the patients’ answers from the questionnaires to extract their psychological profiles. The patients’ profiles are then available to patients and to the clinicians, where the patients wants to share data with his/her clinicians (who could visualize it in multiple alternatives) through the profiler. This knowledge facilitates the patient–clinician relationship in their communications and shared decision-making. The analysis of the results occurs in two phases: the first phase contains the calculation of simple average scores. These scores are calculated because each question’s answers have different weights. The second phase of the analysis contains the calculation of Z-scores, which are more complex, as they consider standard deviations of a healthy group of patients that have already completed the questionnaire. The results of the analysis are provided to the clinicians in multiple alternative visualisations for facilitating their interaction in the decision-making process. This data processing does not produce legal effects or significantly affects the patients.
